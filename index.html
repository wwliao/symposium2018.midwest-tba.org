Overview

Our goals are to provide a platform for the attendees to network and collaborate, and to facilitate individual career development.

The United States has been the world leader for the biotechnology field for at least half a century. The National Institutes of Health (NIH) has about 33 billion budgets per year, with 1.58 million publications funded by it from 1985-2013. Many Taiwanese scientists have been appealed to the US to pursue their career in the biotechnology field. Starting in 2012, several Taiwanese Biotechnology Associations (TBAs) have been founded in Boston, Texas and Southern California. The missions of these TBAs are to strengthen networking among academic and industrial bioscience communities and to foster individual career development. Annual Symposiums are held by these TBAs which have provided numerous opportunities for Taiwanese biotech professionals.

Encouraged by the successful TBAs around the US, Midwest TBA (MTBA) was founded in 2017 by a group of enthusiastic Taiwanese students and researchers from institutions in the Midwest, including Washington University in St. Louis, Stowers Institute, University of Michigan, University of Chicago and Purdue University. We saw the need to connect the biotech professionals in the Midwest since there are many prestigious research institutes and biotech companies in the Midwest. As the former TBAs are located towards the coasts, we believe an annual biotech symposium in the Midwest is strongly needed to provide more opportunities for Taiwanese all across the US.

After months of planning, we have been able to invite multiple successful Taiwanese professionals in both academia and industry as speakers and panelists. We aim to appeal Taiwanese students, postdocs, researchers and professionals in both academia and industry from all over the US, especially the Midwest, to attend our first MTBA symposium this September. Our goals are to provide a platform for the attendees to network and collaborate, and to facilitate individual career development.
